Understanding Eye Diseases

Learn about common eye conditions, their symptoms, and treatments

Cataracts

Clouding of the eye's natural lens, causing blurry vision and glare sensitivity.

Symptoms

  • Cloudy or blurred vision
  • Difficulty seeing at night
  • Sensitivity to light and glare
  • Seeing "halos" around lights

Treatments

Surgery to replace the cloudy lens with an artificial one is the only effective treatment.

Glaucoma

A group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, often due to high eye pressure.

Symptoms

  • Patchy blind spots in peripheral or central vision
  • Tunnel vision in advanced stages
  • Severe headache and eye pain (acute angle-closure glaucoma)
  • Nausea and vomiting

Treatments

Eye drops, oral medications, laser treatment, or surgery to lower eye pressure.

Diabetic Retinopathy

Diabetes complication that affects eyes by damaging blood vessels in the retina.

Symptoms

  • Spots or dark strings floating in vision (floaters)
  • Blurred vision
  • Fluctuating vision
  • Dark or empty areas in vision
  • Vision loss

Treatments

Control of blood sugar, laser treatment, injections, or vitrectomy surgery in advanced cases.

Macular Degeneration

Deterioration of the central portion of the retina (macula) leading to vision loss.

Symptoms

  • Blurred or reduced central vision
  • Difficulty recognizing faces
  • Straight lines appearing wavy
  • Dark, blurry areas in center of vision

Treatments

No cure, but progression can be slowed with vitamins, laser therapy, or injections for wet AMD.

Dry Eye Syndrome

Inadequate tear production or poor tear quality causing eye discomfort.

Symptoms

  • Stinging, burning or scratchy sensation
  • Stringy mucus in or around eyes
  • Eye redness and fatigue
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Difficulty wearing contact lenses

Treatments

Artificial tears, prescription eye drops, punctal plugs, or lifestyle changes to reduce dryness.

Eye Anatomy & Disease Locations

Lens (Cataracts)

The lens focuses light onto the retina. Cataracts cause this normally clear lens to become cloudy.

Optic Nerve (Glaucoma)

The optic nerve carries visual information to the brain. Glaucoma damages this nerve, often due to high eye pressure.

Retina (Diabetic Retinopathy)

The retina converts light into neural signals. Diabetes can damage its blood vessels, causing retinopathy.

Macula (AMD)

The macula provides sharp central vision. Age-related macular degeneration causes deterioration in this area.

Frequently Asked Questions

Adults should have a comprehensive eye exam every 1-2 years, or more frequently if you have diabetes, a family history of eye disease, or other risk factors. Children should have their first eye exam at 6 months, again at 3 years, and before first grade.

While not all eye diseases can be prevented, you can reduce your risk by: wearing UV-protective sunglasses, eating a diet rich in leafy greens and fish, maintaining healthy blood pressure and sugar levels, not smoking, and giving your eyes regular breaks from digital screens.

Our AI system achieves over 95% accuracy for many common eye conditions when compared to specialist diagnoses. However, it's not a substitute for a comprehensive eye exam by a qualified professional. The AI serves as a screening tool to identify potential issues that need further evaluation.

Optometrists (ODs) provide primary vision care including eye exams, vision correction, and diagnosis/treatment of some eye conditions. Ophthalmologists (MDs or DOs) are medical doctors who specialize in eye care, can perform surgery, and treat more complex eye diseases.

Seek immediate care for: sudden vision loss, severe eye pain, sudden onset of flashes/floaters, eye injuries, sudden double vision, or severe redness with pain/nausea. These could indicate serious conditions like retinal detachment, acute glaucoma, or other emergencies.
